Recently, I think SGA is pretty important for our campus. Last semester they helped get extended library hours during Senior week, which was a huge win for everyone. I like that we actually have a way to bring up issues that matter to us students. Sure, sometimes it feels like they move slowly on things, but at least someone is fighting for us when the administration wants to make changes that affect our daily life.

To kind of mixed on SGA. On one hand, it's cool that we have student representation, but sometimes I feel like it's just the same group of people who are already involved in everything else on campus. I went to a few meetings my sophomore year, but it felt like my voice didn't really matter unless I was willing to run for office. I wish there were more ways for regular students to actually influence decisions without having to commit to being in student government.

To be honest, I barely know what SGA does most of the time. I see their posters around campus and get emails about elections, but I don't really feel connected to what they're working on. Maybe they're doing good stuff behind the scenes, but I wish they were better at letting us know what they've actually accomplished. Like, what has changed because of SGA? I'd probably care more if I could see the real impact they're making.
